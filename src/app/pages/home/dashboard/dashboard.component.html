ABOUT TNWESAFE

Tamil Nadu Women Employment & Safety (TNWESAFE) Project is a landmark initiative aimed at fostering women's workforce participation, enhancing their safety, and supporting economic growth in alignment with the State’s vision of becoming a $1 trillion economy by 2030. The project is funded by World Bank and is structured to promote sustainable and inclusive economic development through targeted interventions focused on employment, safety, and empowerment of women

இந்தியாவிலே முதல் முறையாக டாக்டர். கலைஞர் மு. கருணாநிதி அவர்களின் தலைமையில், தமிழக அரசு 1989 ஆம் ஆண்டு, செப்டம்பர் மாதம் தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தில் வேளாண் சார்ந்த தொழில்களில் ஈடுபட்டிருந்த மகளிரைக் கொண்டு.பன்னாட்டு வேளாண்மை வளர்ச்சி நிதி (IFAD) நிதியுதவியுடன் மகளிர் மேம்பாட்டுத் திட்டத்தின் மூலம் சுய உதவிக் குழுக்களை அமைக்க தொடங்கியது.

அடித்தட்டு மக்களின் வாழ்வாதார நிலையை மேம்படுத்துவதாகும்.

அடித்தட்டு மக்களின் வாழ்வாதார நிலையை மேம்படுத்தி, அவர்களின் திறன்களை வளர்த்துத் தன்னிறைவு பெறச் செய்து, பல்வேறு வாழ்வாதாரத் தொழில்களில் அவர்களை வழிநடத்தி ஈடுபடுத்தி, நிலையான வருமானம் கிடைக்கச் செய்து, சமூக மற்றும் பொருளாதார முன்னேற்றத்தை உறுதி செய்வதே அரசாங்கத்தின் முக்கியமானதும் அடிப்படையானதும் ஆன பணியாகும்.

Major Stakeholders

The Tamil Nadu Rural Transformation Project (TNRTP), formerly known as the Vazhndhu Kattuvom Project, is a World Bank–assisted initiative aimed at redefining rural development in Tamil Nadu by moving beyond poverty alleviation and fostering sustainable prosperity for rural communities. Operational across 120 blocks and 3994 village panchayats in 31 districts, the project promotes rural enterprises, strengthens financial inclusion through access to microloans and savings schemes, and focuses on employment creation by equipping individuals—especially women—with the skills and resources needed for economic independence and long-term growth.

The Tamil Nadu Urban Livelihoods Mission (TNULM), launched in 2014–15, is designed to empower and uplift urban poor households in Tamil Nadu by addressing poverty and vulnerability through the formation of strong community organizations. By promoting collective action through Self Help Groups (SHGs), enhancing financial security, encouraging shared decision-making, and equipping individuals with skills and resources for self-employment or skilled wage employment, TNULM supports inclusive urban growth and social security for vulnerable communities.

The Tamil Nadu State Rural Livelihoods Mission (TNSRLM), launched in 2012–13 by the Government of Tamil Nadu, is a comprehensive initiative aimed at empowering rural populations through the formation and strengthening of Self Help Groups (SHGs). The mission focuses on enhancing household incomes and financial security by promoting savings and providing access to microfinance, while also supporting livelihood enhancement through skill development, entrepreneurship, and capacity building. By facilitating market linkages, encouraging community participation, and promoting social inclusion, TNSRLM plays a critical role in building resilience among rural women and enabling holistic, sustainable, and inclusive rural development across the state.

Vision & Mission

Our guiding principles for women’s empowerment and inclusive growth

Our Vision

To build a safe, inclusive, and empowered society where women are central to Tamil Nadu’s growth and development.

  • Empowered Women – Strong Tamil Nadu: Women’s empowerment is a vital growth strategy, not merely a social choice.
  • Safety as a Foundation: Inclusive work environments with zero tolerance for discrimination and violence.
  • Inclusive Growth: Supporting single mothers, transgender individuals, and women from disadvantaged backgrounds to access high-end jobs.

Our Mission

To enable sustainable employment and economic independence for women through targeted interventions and strong institutional support.

  • Enhance Employment: Increase women’s participation in the labor market through skill development, career guidance, and access to quality jobs in emerging and non-farm sectors.
  • Enable Support Systems: Improve access to essential services such as safe workplace transportation, childcare facilities, and secure housing for professional women.

    • Strengthening state capacities and technical partnerships.
